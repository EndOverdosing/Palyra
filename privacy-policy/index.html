Privacy Policy

Effective Date: February 15, 2026

1. Introduction

Welcome to Palyra! This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our secure, decentralized communication platform. By using Palyra, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy. If you do not agree with the terms of this Privacy Policy, please do not access the service.

2. Information We Collect

We collect several types of information to provide and improve our service:

2.1 Account Information

  • Username: A unique identifier you choose when creating your account.
  • Password: Securely hashed and stored using industry-standard encryption.
  • Profile Information: Optional avatar images and profile preferences.

2.2 Communication Data

  • Messages: Text messages sent between users and in servers are stored in our database.
  • Files: Images, videos, and other files you share are stored in our secure storage system.
  • Call Metadata: Call duration and timestamps (not call content).

2.3 Usage Information

  • Device Information: Browser fingerprint, screen resolution, timezone, and platform information for security purposes.
  • Online Status: Last seen timestamps and online/offline status (controllable in settings).
  • Interaction Data: Friend lists, blocked users, server memberships.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information for the following purposes:

  • Service Provision: To create and manage your account, deliver messages, and facilitate communications.
  • Security: To detect and prevent fraud, abuse, bot activity, and unauthorized access through rate limiting and fingerprinting.
  • Feature Enhancement: To provide typing indicators, read receipts, online status, and other real-time features.
  • Notifications: To send push notifications for new messages and friend requests (if enabled).
  • Service Improvement: To analyze usage patterns and improve our platform's performance and features.

4. End-to-End Encryption

Video and Audio Calls: All video and audio calls use peer-to-peer WebRTC technology with end-to-end encryption. Palyra never sees, stores, or has access to your call content. Call data is transmitted directly between participants.

Text Messages: While text messages are stored on our servers to enable features like message history and cross-device sync, we implement strong security measures to protect your data. Future updates may include optional end-to-end encryption for text messages.

5. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information to third parties. We may share information only in the following circumstances:

  • With Your Consent: When you explicitly agree to share information.
  • Service Providers: With trusted third-party services (like Supabase) that help us operate our platform, bound by confidentiality agreements.
  • Legal Requirements: When required by law, subpoena, or other legal process.
  • Safety and Security: To protect the rights, property, or safety of Palyra, our users, or others.

6. Data Storage and Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your data:

  • Passwords are hashed using secure algorithms before storage.
  • All data transmissions use HTTPS/TLS encryption.
  • Database access is restricted and monitored.
  • Regular security audits and updates are performed.
  • Rate limiting and bot detection prevent abuse.

Your data is stored on secure servers provided by Supabase, which complies with SOC 2 Type II standards.

7. Your Privacy Rights and Choices

You have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Access: You can view your account information in settings.
  • Correction: You can update your username and avatar at any time.
  • Deletion: You can request account deletion by contacting support.
  • Privacy Controls: You can disable online status, read receipts, and notifications in settings.
  • Block Users: You can block users to prevent them from contacting you.
  • Export Data: You can request a copy of your data by contacting support.

8. Data Retention

We retain your information as long as your account is active or as needed to provide services. When you delete your account:

  • Your profile and account information are permanently deleted.
  • Your messages remain visible to other participants but are anonymized.
  • Files you uploaded are deleted from our storage within 30 days.
  • Some data may be retained for legal compliance or security purposes for up to 90 days.

9. Third-Party Services

Palyra uses the following third-party services:

  • Supabase: For database, authentication, storage, and real-time functionality. See Supabase Privacy Policy.
  • PeerJS: For peer-to-peer WebRTC signaling. No personal data is shared beyond connection establishment.
  • CDN Providers: For serving static assets like Font Awesome icons.

These services have their own privacy policies, and we encourage you to review them.

10. Children's Privacy

Palyra is not intended for users under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us, and we will delete such information from our systems.

11. International Data Transfers

Your information may be transferred to and maintained on servers located outside of your state, province, country, or other governmental jurisdiction where data protection laws may differ. By using Palyra, you consent to the transfer of your information to these locations.

12. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any significant changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page and updating the "Effective Date" at the top. You are advised to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes. Changes are effective when posted on this page.

13. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, please contact us:

  • Discord: Join our community
  • GitHub: Open an issue
